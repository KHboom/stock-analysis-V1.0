## 8. Cash-Flow Quality

Answer:

- Does net profit convert into cash?
- Is operating cash flow continuously positive?
- Is negative free cash flow due to expansion or weak operations?
- Are receivables eroding cash flow?
- Is inventory at risk of impairment?

## 9. Balance-Sheet Risk

- Cash coverage of short-term debt
- Receivables
- Inventory
- Goodwill
- Equity pledge
- Reductions and unlocks

- Core process capability
- Replacement risk
- Whether R&D is only a reserve without revenue contribution

## 13. Positive Factors

Only include evidence-backed positives, such as policy support, order growth, customer introduction, product ramp, import substitution, profitability improvement, cash-flow improvement, or industry-cycle upturn.

## 14. Risks

- Inventory risk

## 18. Fundamental Signals To Wait For

This is still not buy-point analysis. Possible signals:

- Next report confirms revenue growth
- Recurring profit improves
- Operating cash flow turns positive
- Major order is announced
- Customer introduction is confirmed
- Gross margin stabilizes
- Receivables decline
- Inventory is digested
- Valuation returns to a reasonable range
- Inquiry letter or risk event is resolved
