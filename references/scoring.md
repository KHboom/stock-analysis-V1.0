# Scoring And Rating

Use this 100-point framework after industry relevance, financial quality, industry position, and valuation checks.

## 1. Policy And Industry Space: 20 Points

| Item | Points |
|---|---:|
| Fits national strategic direction | 5 |
| Industry space is large enough | 5 |
| The next 3-5 years are in construction or volume-ramp stage | 4 |
| Import substitution or security-development logic | 3 |
| Policy catalysts are persistent | 3 |

Guide:

- 16-20: strong policy, large space, clear ramp path
- 11-15: clear policy support, uncertain monetization pace
- 6-10: policy theme exists, commercialization remains distant
- 0-5: weak policy relevance

## 2. Company Competitiveness: 20 Points

| Item | Points |
|---|---:|
| Core industry-chain position | 5 |
| Technical barriers | 4 |
| Customer quality | 4 |
| Order or supply-chain verification | 4 |
| Scale or cost advantage | 3 |

Guide:

- 16-20: core link, high-quality customers, clear orders
- 11-15: real business, competitive edge not fully verified
- 6-10: marginal beneficiary with ordinary barriers
- 0-5: concept mapping dominates

## 3. Financial Quality: 25 Points

| Item | Points |
|---|---:|
| Revenue growth quality | 5 |
| Recurring net profit quality | 5 |
| Gross margin and net margin stability | 4 |
| ROE / ROIC | 4 |
| R&D effectiveness | 3 |
| Expense control | 2 |
| Profit sustainability | 2 |

Guide:

- 20-25: revenue, recurring profit, and profitability improve together
- 15-19: growth is good but has some flaws
- 8-14: unstable growth or ordinary profit quality
- 0-7: revenue and profit quality are clearly insufficient

## 4. Valuation Reasonableness: 15 Points

| Item | Points |
|---|---:|
| Current valuation matches growth | 5 |
| Historical valuation percentile | 3 |
| Peer valuation comparison | 3 |
| Market value matches orders/revenue | 2 |
| Expectations are not obviously overdrawn | 2 |

Guide:

- 12-15: valuation broadly matches growth
- 8-11: valuation is somewhat high but explainable by growth
- 4-7: valuation is clearly expensive
- 0-3: valuation is seriously overdrawn

Note: scoring above 10/15 requires all three valuation dimensions to be present — historical percentile, peer comparison using a consistent peer set, and earnings sensitivity analysis. A valuation section missing any one of these is capped at 10/15 regardless of other merits.

## 5. Cash Flow And Balance-Sheet Safety: 10 Points

| Item | Points |
|---|---:|
| Operating cash-flow quality | 3 |
| Free cash-flow trend | 2 |
| Debt ratio and interest-bearing debt | 2 |
| Receivable and inventory risk | 2 |
| Goodwill, pledge, reduction, and other risks | 1 |

Guide:

- 8-10: healthy cash flow and safe liabilities
- 5-7: some pressure but controllable
- 2-4: cash flow and balance sheet are weak
- 0-1: obvious financial risk

## 6. Market Structure And Technical Interface: 10 Points

This module is an interface for later technical analysis, not a buy/sell decision inside this skill.

| Item | Points |
|---|---:|
| No obvious theme crowding | 3 |
| Price has not substantially overdrawn expectations | 3 |
| Volume and institutional attention are not abnormal | 2 |
| No large unlock, reduction, or pledge pressure | 2 |

Guide:

- 8-10: trading structure is relatively healthy
- 5-7: high heat; wait for pullback or financial validation
- 2-4: crowding and volatility risk are obvious
- 0-1: speculation or risk events are concentrated

## Hard Exclusions

Directly rate D when any of these are present:

- Financial fraud, major accounting error, or major regulatory penalty
- Abnormal audit opinion
- Consecutive losses with deteriorating cash flow
- High controlling-shareholder pledge plus frequent reduction
- Major debt default or liquidity crisis
- Core business continues to deteriorate while relying on concept speculation
- Related industry revenue cannot be verified
- Major goodwill impairment risk remains unresolved
- Frequent refinancing without core-business improvement
- Major litigation, penalty, or inquiry issue remains unresolved

## Mandatory Downgrades

Downgrade at least one level when these issues are material:

- Receivables grow faster than revenue for consecutive periods
- Inventory increases sharply without clear order support
- Operating cash flow is continuously weaker than net profit
- Gross margin declines continuously
- Customer concentration is excessive
- Capitalized R&D ratio is abnormal
- Growth mainly depends on government subsidy or investment income
- Executives leave frequently
- Large unlock is near
- Valuation is much higher than peers without enough earnings delivery

## Rating

| Rating | Score | Meaning | Handling |
|---|---:|---|---|
| A: Strong tracking | >=80 | Strong relevance, good financial quality, valuation not obviously overdrawn | Core observation pool |
| B: Cautious tracking | 65-79 | Fundamental highlights but valuation, cash flow, or delivery risk remains | Normal observation pool |
| C: Not considered for now | 50-64 | Business relevance or financial quality is insufficient | Not a priority |
| D: High-risk avoidance | <50 or hard exclusion | High financial, governance, valuation, or concept risk | Avoid |

A does not mean buy. B means wait for clearer reports, orders, or valuation repair. C should not be upgraded because of short-term heat. D is unsuitable for a small account.
